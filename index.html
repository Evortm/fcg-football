How We Train

FCG follows a structured, long-term development method. We do not train random drills. Each session has a clear football problem, a small number of focus points, and exercises that move from simple repetition to realistic pressure.

1

Clear Weekly Topics

Every week has a football theme, so players and coaches know the main learning objective. Players work through attacking and defending topics such as building up, attacking the wings, scoring from crosses, pressing, defending wide areas, and protecting the goal.

2

Focus Points

Coaches use a small number of focus points per session so players know exactly what to improve: body position, first touch, receiving angle, timing, communication, reaction, or finishing technique.

3

Game-Realistic Training

Exercises start simple, then add pressure, opponents, direction, transition, and match speed. The goal is not only to perform an action, but to use it at the right moment in a real game.

4

Positive Coaching

We want players to enjoy football, work hard, be brave, and learn from mistakes. Confidence and motivation are part of development. Players are tracked across technical, tactical, physical, communication, and mindset areas.
